1. Overall Audit Verdict
Audit Scope & Evidence Base

This report presents the combined findings of a Type 1 System Effectiveness Audit (SEA) and Type 2 Performance & Competence Evaluation (PCE) conducted on the Maintenance Department, covering Core Operations, Paid Services, and Warranty sub-processes (May 2026, GASD-IAF-001 v2.0, ISO 9001:2015).

Teams audited: four active service Maintenance Teams. Evidence sources: direct site observations, Odoo ticketing records, worker interviews, WhatsApp logs, cancelled and on-hold ticket analysis (141 tickets), Maintenance SOW v2, and Terms & Conditions across Faham products.

Section 2.5 — Field Evidence Authenticity: The field-verification data originates from an independent, structured field-performance audit. Thirty-one (31) unique field deviations were captured via direct, live-site observation — 25 Critical-risk and 6 Non-Critical-risk — forming the primary, unedited empirical baseline. The complete granular data, timestamps, and Odoo logs are maintained under version control in the master evidence repository.

1.2 Executive Conclusion — Governance Failure, Not a Performance Issue

In the Lead Auditor's professional opinion, the deficiencies recorded are not the result of isolated individual underperformance. They are the predictable outcome of a service-delivery system operating without the foundational governance infrastructure required by ISO 9001:2015 — defined processes, clear accountability, competence assurance, and controlled, evidence-based execution.

#Structural FailureDescription
1Lack of Accountability Chain & Misaligned Role Competency in Field TeamsNo designated team leader provides documented oversight, escalation, or performance monitoring. The Driver — responsible for supervising the carpenter's work, client communication, reporting, and documentation — lacks the technical knowledge to oversee carpentry or Faham product tasks. The Carpenter shows inconsistent, limited skills in specialised areas (smart locks, wall-fit systems, pivot doors).
2Maintenance Absorbing Upstream FailuresThe department functions as a corrective mechanism for installation defects, unauthorised field modifications, and poor handovers. Without robust control gates at installation and handover, maintenance remains overburdened with avoidable rework, limiting capacity to support new revenue-generating activities.
3Focus on Speed Over QualityService performance is measured primarily by ticket-closure speed rather than first-time-fix rate or quality outcomes. This incentive structure has fostered unjustified cancellations, incomplete execution, and recurring visits — all increasing the Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ) and eroding brand reputation.
4On-Hold Backlog as a Fourth Structural FailureA total of 141 tickets are On-Hold, all assigned to a single individual, none with a documented hold reason — creating SLA and regulatory exposure invisible to management.
5Improper and Premature Ticket ClosuresTickets are closed or cancelled under arbitrary justifications without exhausting investigation or escalation; critical quality defects are dismissed without root cause analysis, follow-up, or recorded notification.
"The solution lies not in adding more resources, but in implementing structured governance, clear accountability, and enforceable quality standards across the entire service delivery chain."

11.2 Meeting Summary — Eight Field-Team Concerns
#ThemeVoice of the Field Team
1Lack of Employee RecognitionTeam members do not feel adequately appreciated or recognised for their efforts and contributions.
2High Workload & Unrealistic ExpectationsTasks assigned with impractical timeframes (e.g. 2 doors in 1 hour). Constant follow-up calls for pending complaints add concurrent pressure.
3Non-Emergency Case EscalationsField teams are instructed to attend non-urgent cases mid-schedule, disrupting planned work and priorities.
4Extended Working HoursTeams frequently work beyond 5 PM with no fixed finish time. Distant sites are assigned the following day after late finishes, impacting rest and recovery.
5Communication & Coordination ChallengesTeams are redirected between multiple coordinators when seeking assistance, causing delays and frustration.
6Overtime & Welfare ConcernsWork extending beyond normal hours yields no overtime compensation or meal support for field teams.
7Material Management IssuesMaterials are sometimes retained with field teams for future use due to receiving incorrect materials from coordination. Coordination teams are perceived as not understanding site requirements.
8Scope Expansion at Customer SitesTechnicians are frequently asked to inspect additional doors beyond the original SOW on arrival. Reluctance to refuse customers results in overrun and delays; coordinators are perceived as not accounting for this in planning.
